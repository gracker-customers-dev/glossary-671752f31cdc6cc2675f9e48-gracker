BOTS

Automated software applications that perform tasks over the internet.

Description

In the context of cybersecurity, 'BOTS' refers to automated software applications that execute tasks over the internet, often without human intervention. While many bots serve legitimate purposes, such as web crawlers that index content for search engines or customer service chatbots, there is a darker side to their usage. Malicious bots can be used to perform a variety of nefarious activities, including spamming, data scraping, and launching Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks. These bots are typically part of a larger network known as a 'botnet,' where compromised devices are controlled by an attacker to execute coordinated attacks or tasks. Cybersecurity professionals must implement measures to detect and mitigate the impact of these malicious bots to protect networks and data. The growth of the Internet of Things (IoT) has further expanded the potential for bot-related threats, as more devices are connected and can be exploited to form botnets.

Examples

  • DDoS attack bots that flood websites with traffic to overwhelm them.
  • Web scraping bots that extract data from web pages without permission.

Additional Information

  • Bots can be classified as 'good' (helpful) or 'bad' (malicious).
  • Botnets can consist of thousands or even millions of infected devices.

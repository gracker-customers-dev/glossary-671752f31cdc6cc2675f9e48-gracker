Threat Intelligence - TAXII

TAXII (Trusted Automated eXchange of Indicator Information) is a protocol for sharing cyber threat intelligence in a structured way.

Description

TAXII is a set of specifications developed to enable the automated exchange of cyber threat intelligence (CTI) among organizations. It is designed to facilitate the sharing of indicators of compromise (IoCs), threat data, and other relevant information in a standardized format. TAXII operates over HTTP and provides a means for organizations to communicate security-related data in real-time, enhancing their ability to respond to threats. The protocol allows for the establishment of a client-server architecture where threat intelligence can be requested, shared, or published to various stakeholders, including security operations centers (SOCs) and incident response teams. By leveraging TAXII, organizations can improve their situational awareness, coordinate their defenses, and reduce the time to detect and respond to cyber threats. TAXII is often used in conjunction with the Structured Threat Information Expression (STIX), which provides a language for describing CTI in a machine-readable format, thereby enabling more effective automated processing of threat data.

Examples

  • An organization uses TAXII to share IoCs with its partners after detecting a new malware strain.
  • A government agency publishes threat intelligence findings using TAXII to inform private sector companies about emerging threats.

Additional Information

  • TAXII is maintained by the OASIS Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Technical Committee.
  • It is widely adopted in the cybersecurity community, allowing for interoperability between different security tools and platforms.

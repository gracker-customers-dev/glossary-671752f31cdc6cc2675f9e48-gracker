SPYWARE

Spyware is a type of malicious software designed to gather information about a person or organization without their consent.

Description

Spyware operates by secretly installing itself on a victim's device, often without the user's knowledge. Once installed, it can monitor user activities, track keystrokes, capture sensitive information such as passwords and credit card numbers, and relay this data back to the attacker. Spyware can also take control of the system's microphone and camera, allowing the attacker to listen and watch the user. This type of malware is often bundled with legitimate software or distributed through deceptive websites, emails, or downloads. Users may not even realize they have spyware on their devices until they notice unusual behavior or performance issues. Because it operates covertly, spyware poses a significant risk to personal privacy and cybersecurity. Organizations and individuals are advised to use anti-spyware tools and maintain good security practices to detect and remove spyware before it can cause significant harm.

Examples

  • Keyloggers: Capture every keystroke made by the user to gather passwords and sensitive data.
  • Adware: A form of spyware that tracks user's browsing habits to deliver targeted advertisements.

Additional Information

  • Spyware can significantly slow down device performance and cause crashes.
  • Regularly updating software and using reputable antivirus programs can help prevent spyware infections.
